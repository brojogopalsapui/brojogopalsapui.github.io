Research

Research themes

My research is centered on how the security and trustworthiness of AI systems change when they are implemented in real hardware and deployed in edge and physical environments.

AI security across layers

Bridging model-level vulnerabilities with implementation-aware attack surfaces in cloud, edge, and physical systems.

Hardware trust

Studying side-channel leakage, fault effects, accelerator exposure, and circuit-level resilience.

Edge and physical AI

Understanding how sensing, timing, communication, control, and physical access change the security problem.

Emerging devices and co-design

Security-aware design directions spanning resilient circuits, flexible or emerging technologies, and device-to-system integration.

Representative directions

  • Side-channel analysis and fault injection against AI accelerators
  • Hyperdimensional and neuromorphic computing security
  • Robust and efficient design for printed or emerging-device neuromorphic circuits
  • Threat modeling and cross-layer countermeasures for edge and physical AI
  • Hardware-aware trust for embodied and deployment-constrained intelligent systems
