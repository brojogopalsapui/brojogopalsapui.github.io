Research

I focus on cross-layer physical security for AI hardware—linking device/circuit behavior to microarchitecture and algorithm-level leakage and robustness.

Core Areas

  • Side-channel analysis (SCA): power/EM/timing leakage in AI accelerators and similarity-search engines.
  • Fault injection (FI): voltage/clock/glitch-based perturbations to induce misclassification or reveal secrets.
  • Secure AI accelerators: architecture-aware defenses (masking, randomization, robust design flows).
  • Emerging devices & co-design: security-aware characterization using device knobs such as back-bias / rFET behavior.

Representative Questions

  • What leaks during similarity computations (e.g., Hamming distance, popcount, MAC)?
  • Which time-windows are most fault-sensitive, and why?
  • How to design measurable, silicon-validated countermeasures?

Methods & Tooling

  • FPGA prototyping (triggered trace capture, profiling/attacks)
  • ChipWhisperer fault injection workflows
  • Device/circuit simulation & PVT/corner analysis
  • Security evaluation with reproducible scripts
